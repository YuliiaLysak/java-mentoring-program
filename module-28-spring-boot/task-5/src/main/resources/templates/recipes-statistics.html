Key Cooking Statistics and Facts

  • About 36% of Americans cook at home on a daily basis.
  • 13.7% of those surveyed prepare their meals at home because of a strong passion for cooking.
  • As a result of the pandemic, Americans are both cooking (54%) and baking (46%) more.
  • UK citizens reluctantly spend time in the kitchen—and takeout app orders seem to have tripled between 2013 and 2018.
  • Culinary Tablets kept at Yale, dating from 1700 BCE, are the world’s oldest documented recipes.
  • Some celebratory pies served in 16th century England contained live animals.
  • According to the cooking statistics, 50% of Americans became eager to learn about cooking during the Coronavirus pandemic.
  • Women who prefer to cook from scratch after gaining recipe information on food TV shows tend to have a higher BMI than those who don’t.
  • There’s a type of fish served in Japan that requires a chef to get a special license before being allowed to serve it.
  • American pandemic cooking includes more and more seafood.
